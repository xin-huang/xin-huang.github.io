Here, I summarize a checklist when modeling in population genetics.

<!-- more -->

- Which kind of models: deterministic or stochastic?
- Which kind of time views: forward or backward (coalescent)?
- Which kind of phenomenons: dynamic or static (equilibrium)?
- Which kind of generations: discrete or continuous?
- Which kind of forces: drift, mating, mutation, migration, or selection?
- Which kind of mating: random, inbreeding/introgression, assortative or disassortative?
- Which kind of gene relation: no interaction, (physical) linkage or epistasis?
- Which kind of dominance: no dominance, complete or partial?
- Which kind of fitnesses: constant or variable?
- Which kind of frameworks: maximum likelihood or Bayesian?
- How many loci: single or multiple?
- How many populations: single or multiple?
- How many sexes affected: one or both?
