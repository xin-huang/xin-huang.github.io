My research interests:

  • The evolution of complex traits
    A complex trait—such as the color of human skin, hair, and eye—is a trait that is affected by many genes. How complex traits evolve is a fundamental problem in biology. I have proposed a new approach to quantify how selective pressures shape the diversity of human pigmentation during different time periods. I would like to develop new models and apply genetic data to investigate how different demographic forces drive the evolution of complex traits. I also would like to explore different footprints of evolution—such as sexual selection and convergent evolution—in complex traits.
  • The development of tools for large-scale datasets
    As the amount of genomic data has increased dramatically, research community requires more efficient tools for analyzing massive genomic datasets. I have implemented such a tool called SeleDiff, which is fast and scalable for estimating and testing selection differences between populations. I would like to continue developing tools that would accelerate the discovery of potential causal variants in evolution.
