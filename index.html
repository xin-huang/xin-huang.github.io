Xin Huang

Currently, I completed my Ph.D. in the laboratory of Professor Li Jin at CAS-MPG Partner Institute for Computational Biology.
My main research area is population genetics/genomics. I am very interested in:

  • The evolution of complex traits
    The color variation of human skin, hair and eye is a highly diverse trait among populations. Its obvious diversity has drawn particular attention from both scientists and non-scientists for thousands of years, as noted by Charles Darwin one century ago and as noticed by ancient Egyptians more than 4000 years ago. Although previous studies have shown several pigmentation genes are under natural selection, it is still unclear about the intensity of natural selection on these genes between different populations and epochs.
  • The development of tools for large-scale datasets in population genomics
    As the amount of genomic data has increased dramatically, research community requires more efficient tools for analyzing massive genomic datasets. I have implemented such a tool called SeleDiff, which is fast and scalable for estimating and testing selection differences between populations. I would like to continue developing tools that would accelerate the discovery of potential causal variants in evolution.
